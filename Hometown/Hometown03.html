Kundelungu National Park

Kundelungu National Park , also called Kundelungu National Park , is a national park in Katanga in the Democratic Republic of the Congo . Established during the colonial era, it received the status of a national park by ordinances law (no) 70-317 ofNovember 30, 1970and (no) 75-097 ofMarch 1 , 1975. Covering an area of ​​7,600 km², the park presents a succession of high plateaus and savannah hills ranging between 1,200 and 1,700 meters above sea level. It owes its fame to the falls of the Lofoï , one of the tributaries of the Lufira . These falls are in fact the highest in Africa and the second in the world, with a height of 384 m and a continuous jet of water of 347 m.
