National Museum of Lubumbashi


The Lubumbashi National Museum is a museum in the Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC), located in Lubumbashi , in the southeast of the country. It is attached to the Institute of National Museums of Congo (IMNC 1 ) under the aegis of the Ministry of Culture and the Arts.

History

Founded in 1958, the “Élisabethville Museum” successively took the name “Léopold II Museum” (or “Cabu Museum”, in reference to its founder, Doctor Francis Cabu), “Katanga Regional Museum” in the 1960s, before becoming the “National Museum of Lubumbashi” in 19702.
